# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads define and execute the quality strategy across planning, implementation, and release. They coordinate testing activities and provide release signoff recommendations based on quality criteria.

### Responsibilities
- Define test strategy and quality gates for each release
- Develop and maintain test plans across functional, integration, and regression coverage
- Coordinate manual and automated testing activities with Developers and testers
- Track, triage, and communicate quality risks and blockers
- Provide go/no-go recommendation for release readiness with the Project Manager

### Goals
- Prevent critical defects from reaching production
- Increase confidence in release quality and stability
- Maintain clear, repeatable quality processes across teams

### Interactions with existing roles
- **Developers:** Align on acceptance criteria, test coverage, defect triage, and fix verification
- **Product Managers:** Validate feature behavior against requirements and user expectations
- **Project Managers:** Align on test timelines, quality risks, and release signoff decisions

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers ensure product usability, accessibility, and design consistency. They collaborate with delivery teams to translate user needs into intuitive experiences.

### Responsibilities
- Create and refine wireframes, prototypes, and UI specifications
- Define and review accessibility and usability requirements
- Collaborate with Product Managers on user flows and journey validation
- Partner with Developers and QA Leads to ensure design intent is implemented correctly
- Support design QA during execution and pre-release validation

### Goals
- Deliver user-first features that are intuitive and accessible
- Reduce rework caused by unclear or inconsistent design decisions
- Improve user satisfaction and adoption outcomes

### Interactions with existing roles
- **Developers:** Clarify implementation details, edge cases, and component behavior
- **Product Managers:** Align design direction to product goals, priorities, and user feedback
- **Project Managers:** Coordinate timelines for design deliverables and review checkpoints

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers own deployment orchestration and release communication. They coordinate rollout, validation, and rollback readiness across teams.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and coordinate release windows and deployment sequencing
- Prepare and publish release notes with stakeholder-facing summaries
- Confirm rollback plans and operational readiness before deployment
- Coordinate go-live communication and post-release status reporting
- Drive release retrospectives for deployment and process improvements

### Goals
- Deliver predictable, low-risk releases
- Minimize downtime and release-related incidents
- Ensure stakeholders are informed before, during, and after release

### Interactions with existing roles
- **Developers:** Coordinate deployment readiness, build artifacts, and production checks
- **Product Managers:** Align release content and customer-facing messaging with priorities
- **Project Managers:** Align release timing, milestone commitments, and escalation paths

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create and maintain clear user-facing and internal documentation. They help teams capture decisions, processes, and usage guidance consistently.

### Responsibilities
- Produce and maintain user guides, process docs, and release documentation
- Standardize structure, terminology, and quality of project documentation
- Collaborate with delivery teams to document features, workflows, and key decisions
- Identify documentation gaps during planning, execution, and release
- Support onboarding by keeping core process and reference docs current

### Goals
- Improve clarity and discoverability of project knowledge
- Reduce onboarding time and repeated clarification requests
- Keep documentation aligned with shipped behavior and process changes

### Interactions with existing roles
- **Developers:** Gather technical details and implementation context for docs
- **Product Managers:** Align documentation tone and content with product intent and audience
- **Project Managers:** Synchronize documentation deliverables with milestones and release timelines
