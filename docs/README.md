# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome! This folder is the central guide to how OctoAcme plans, executes, and improves project delivery. If you are new to the team, start with the overview below and then open the linked guides based on your role and current project phase.

OctoAcme employs a structured, yet adaptable, project management approach that emphasizes clear roles, iterative delivery, and strong communication channels. At the heart of its process is the definition of core personas: Project Managers (PMs), Product Managers (PdMs), Developers, QA/Testers, and Stakeholders. Each role is distinguished by specific responsibilities and goals—PMs handle logistics, risk, and alignment; PdMs steer the product vision and prioritization; Developers focus on building and testing features; QA/Testers safeguard quality; and Stakeholders offer input and approvals.

Workflows are broken down along the typical project lifecycle, from initiation—where a one-pager captures the problem, goals, and stakeholders—to planning, execution, release, and retrospectives. Projects move through defined activities such as stakeholder kickoffs, backlog prioritization, release planning, and risk assessment. Execution is supported by rhythms like daily standups, weekly delivery syncs, and regular demos, all tracked transparently on GitHub project boards with well-defined states (Backlog, Ready, In Progress, In Review, QA, Done).

Communication is both formal and adaptive, combining scheduled syncs (weekly PM+PdM, stakeholder updates, team standups) with ad-hoc escalation paths. Templates ensure consistency for weekly status reports and incident communications, while a clear escalation framework routes blockers from the team level up to sponsors for resolution. All communication focuses on alignment, transparency, and preserving a psychologically safe space for feedback and learning.

Quality assurance is integrated throughout the process. Automated testing and linting in CI pipelines are mandatory before code reviews or merges; teams employ a mix of unit, integration, and smoke tests, with security scans supplementing quality gates. Manual QA is performed for feature acceptance when necessary, while retrospective meetings and continuous improvement activities ensure systematic learning and adaptation after each milestone, release, or incident. This holistic approach enables OctoAcme to deliver value iteratively, manage risk proactively, and maintain a culture of continuous improvement.
